Err_404 1.0, a 24-hour offline state-level hackathon held in October 2017, attracted 160 registered teams, out of which 60 were selected to compete.

The winning team, "Rockstar Daemons" from St. John College of Engineering, was awarded a cash prize of ₹20,000 along with a trophy. The second-place went to "JSCOE IOT Club" from Jayawantrao Sawant College of Engineering, and the third-place was secured by "Team Anton" from M.H.S.S.C.E, Mumbai, or "Campus Connect" from V.J.T.I. Mumbai. Additionally, the title of the best female hacker team was claimed by "Team Warrior" from M.H.S.S.C.E, Mumbai.

Err_404 2.0

The ERR_404 2.0 was a 24-hour offline state-level hackathon held in March 2019. The winning team received a cash prize of ₹30,000 along with a trophy and other goodies. A total of 108 teams registered for the event, out of which 56 teams were selected to participate.

The winning teams were as follows: 1st place was secured by CSP22 from M.H.S.S.C.E, Mumbai, winning ₹15,000; 2nd place went to ImagiNation from Vidyavardhini's College of Engineering & Technology, earning ₹10,000; 3rd place was awarded to InoApk.ai from SIES Graduate School Of Technology, with a prize of ₹5,000. Additionally, the best female hacker team, House Stack from Fr. Conceicao Rodrigues College Of Engineering, was recognized for their exceptional performance.

The ERR_404 3.0, a 24-hour offline state-level hackathon held in February-March 2020, saw a total of 93 teams registering for the event. Out of these, 52 teams were selected to participate.

The winning team, Yare Yare Lulz, secured the first position and received a cash prize of ₹25,000 along with a trophy and other goodies. The second-place team, Reactive Error, was awarded ₹10,000, and the third-place team, Cortex, received ₹5,000. Additionally, a special recognition was given to the best female hacker team, Team Vier.

Err_404 4.0

The Err_404 4.0 was a 24-hour virtual international-level hackathon that took place in March 2021. The event saw enthusiastic participation with a total of 89 teams registered, and 68 teams were selected to compete.

The winning teams were awarded cash prizes, with the 1st place going to "Byt3-Forc-3" from Vidyalankar Institute of Technology, securing ₹25,000. The 2nd place was claimed by "TLE" from Veermata Jijabai Technological Institute, winning ₹15,000, and the 3rd place was awarded to "Adroits" from M.H Saboo Siddik College of Engineering, receiving ₹8,000. Additionally, the "PyGirls" from Thadomal Shahani College of Engineering were recognized as the best female hacker team in the competition. Overall, it was an exciting hackathon event with notable achievements and rewards for the participating teams.

Err_404 5.0

The err_404 5.0, a 36-hour offline national-level hackathon held in March 2023, featured a substantial prize pool of ₹2.5 Lakhs. The event attracted 131 registered teams, with 54 teams ultimately selected to compete.

Notable winners included the 1st place team, InnovateHERs from Thadomal Shahani Engineering College, who secured ₹25,000, while the 2nd place went to Silicon Stallions from Anjuman-I-Islam's Kalsekar Technical Campus, earning ₹15,000. The 3rd prize of ₹8,000 was claimed by HEVC, also from Thadomal Shahani Engineering College. Additionally, Women Of Worth (WOW) from Saraswati College of Engineering were recognized as the best female hacker team, receiving ₹3,000.
